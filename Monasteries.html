The Serene Monasteries of Sikkim

Discover the spiritual heart of Sikkim. Explore ancient monasteries, witness serene rituals, and immerse yourself in the profound tranquility of these sacred spaces.

Rumtek Monastery

Near Gangtok

One of the largest and most significant monasteries in Sikkim, Rumtek is the seat of the Karmapa Lama. It's a fine example of Tibetan Buddhist architecture.

location_on 24 km from Gangtok

Pemayangtse Monastery

West Sikkim

Founded in the 17th century, this monastery is one of the oldest in Sikkim. It houses a remarkable collection of ancient scriptures and religious artworks.

location_on 110 km from Gangtok, near Pelling

Tashiding Monastery

West Sikkim

Perched on a heart-shaped hill, Tashiding is considered one of the holiest monasteries. It's famous for the Bhumchu festival, where a sacred pot of water predicts the state's future.

location_on 40 km from Pelling

Enchey Monastery

Gangtok

Meaning 'the solitary temple', Enchey Monastery is an important seat of the Nyingma order. It is believed to be blessed by Lama Druptob Karpo, a master of tantric art.

location_on 3 km from main city Gangtok

Ralang Monastery

South Sikkim

Known for its impressive collection of paintings and thangkas, Ralang Monastery hosts the annual Pang Lhabsol festival with vibrant masked dances.

location_on 6 km from Ravangla

Phodong Monastery

North Sikkim

One of the six main monasteries in Sikkim, Phodong houses beautiful murals, and is a significant center for the Kagyupa sect.

location_on 38 km from Gangtok
